The Need for Local Environmental Action

Key Environmental Challenges:

1. Pollution:

  • Air pollution from vehicles, industries, and burning fossil fuels contributes to climate change and respiratory diseases.
  • Water pollution from plastic waste, industrial discharge, and agricultural runoff harms marine life and contaminates drinking water.
  • Soil pollution from pesticides, chemicals, and improper waste disposal depletes soil fertility and affects food safety.

2. Deforestation:

  • Around 10 million hectares of forests are lost every year due to urbanization, agriculture, and illegal logging.
  • Leads to habitat destruction, endangering species and reducing biodiversity.
  • Reduces carbon sequestration, increasing greenhouse gas levels and accelerating climate change.

3. Waste Management Issues:

  • Over 2 billion tons of municipal solid waste is generated globally each year, with only about 20% properly recycled.
  • Plastic waste takes hundreds of years to decompose, polluting oceans and harming wildlife.
  • Electronic waste (e-waste) is rising rapidly, with toxic substances contaminating land and water.
